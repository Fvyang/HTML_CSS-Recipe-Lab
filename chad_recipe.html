Fried Chicken has been a staple of dining tables and restraurants across the United States. Often the most important features to what makes great fried chicken are how crispy/crunchy and moist they are.

However, fried chicken can be some of the most challenging to master. Anything that could go wrong can happen such as being: burnt, dry, not crispy. Not to worry though, here is a simple recipe that will ensure that your chicken will be flavorful, moist & crispy/crunchy to whatever your heart desires.

Let's get started.


Recipe (Ingredients)

  • 2 lbs (24oz) of chicken
  • 1 cup (8oz) of flour
  • 1 cup (8oz) of corn starch
  • 2 cups (16oz) of buttermilk
  • 1.5 tbsp of salt (1 tbsp for brine; the rest for seasoning mixture)
  • 1.5 tsp of pepper (1 tbsp for brine; the rest for seasoning mixture)
  • 1/2 tsp of paprika
  • 1 tbsp of onion powder
  • 1 tbsp of garlic powder
  • 1 tsp of sugar
  • Canola or peanut oil
Optional items for preparation:
  • 1 liter bottle of sparkling water
  • 1/2 tsp of cayenne pepper

Directions

You may completely skip the preparation section, though the chicken may not be as moist and tender as anticipated.

Preparation time (12-24hrs):

  1. Take out the 2 lbs of chicken and thoroughly rinse them.
  2. In a large mixing bowl or container, combine the chicken, sparkling water (enough to submerge all the chicken), and 1 tbsp of salt.
  3. Seal/cover the mixing bowl/container and put into a fridge for 12-24 hours.
  4. In a separate bowl, pour in and mix the flour, corn starch, salt, black pepper, onion powder, garlic powder, paprika, cayenne pepper, and sugar. Cover and put it aside for later.

After 12-24 hours, the fun begins...

  1. Take out the 2 lbs of chicken in the mixing bowl/container from the fridge and empty out all the sparkling water.
  2. Sprinkle 1 tsp of salt & 1 tsp of black pepper on the chicken.
  3. Proceed to evently coat all the chicken with (up to 16 oz) buttermilk.
  4. One-by-one, take one piece of buttermilk-coated chicken and lightly dip it/dredge into the prepared dry seasoning mixture. Set them on a rack/tray.
  5. Pour the canola/peanut oil into the pot/frying pan and heat it to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). This step will allow time for the chicken to soak in with the buttermilk and batter.
  6. Carefully lower the coated chicken pieces into the pot/pan. All pieces should be fully submerged and spaced apart in frying oil.
  7. Let the chicken cook untouched for about 10 minutes (for wings, drummies) or 12 minutes (for legs, thighs, breasts). Ensure the oil temperature is maintained, as the inserted chicken will lower it.
  8. Once skin is deep golden brown and an instant-read thermometer inserted into thickest part of chicken registers at 165Â°, take them out and place them into a serving plate. Repeat steps 6-8 with additional batches until all finished.
  9. Let the chicken cool off for 5 minutes, serve, and enjoy all the flavorful, moist, crispy, goodness these glorious wings have to offer.

And there you have it. You've successfully created some of the most delicious fried chicken on earth. Feel free to adjust the spices, as well as the ratio of the batter mix (for example, 75% corn starch & 25% flour for more crispness) to your linking. Also, you can skip the brining to save prep time, though you may sacrafice some moistness and flavor of the chicken.
